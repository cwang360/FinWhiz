How Does This Work?

A Monte Carlo simulation uses historical data to simulate future trends. Hundreds of simulations are run, and the end results of all the simulations are used to determine the probability of each result. The distribution graph shows the distribution of all results from the simulations, and the average price (center of distribution) has the highest probability of occurring. If enough simulations are run to form a normal distribution, the average and standard deviation of all simulation results can be used to generate a probability for any result.

Note: The simulation and calculations are run on Wolfram-Alpha's servers each time the submit button is clicked, so only 100 simulations are run (to reduce the time needed to display results), which may cause the distribution to be slightly different from a normal distribution. Also, since the calculations are run again each time, results may vary slightly for the same company and future prediction each time "submit" is clicked, but they should be nearly the same.
